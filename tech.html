BRIDGE IT
Building model bridges is fun. So fun that sometimes you might not even know you are learning. Physics, math, and engineering concepts are brought to life through the hands-on project of designing and building a model bridge.


So, are you going to accept this CHALLENGE?
General Rules:
1)Bridge should be of the type: Suspension Bridge.
2)Participation will be in the teams of 2.
3)Material will be provided to the participants.
4)Bridges should be made from Popsicle sticks and fevicol only.
5)Adhesives expect fevicol are not allowed.
6)Only 4 supports should be added on the opposite corners.
7)A bridge that does not support itself will comply to disqualification
ROUND 1:
It is a prelim round, in which the teams are asked to pass the Quiz which is related to the Civil Engineering.
And the 5 teams are qualified to the next round and they have to make the bridge.
ROUND 2:
In this round the team are provided with the popsicle sticks,fevicol and clips


Judging Criteria:
1)Self weight of the bridge (The minimum, the better).
2)Load will be added on the centre of the bridge till it collapses. If the bridge does not collapse till the maximum weight, the deflection will be measured.
3)Aesthetics.
4)Marks will be deducted if there is increase or decrease in length 5mm or onwards.
5)Marks will be deducted if number of sticks used will be increase from 250

CAD WARS (AutoCAD)
Here's a 3 pointer for all-you-need-to-know about AutoCAD!
CAD stands for Computer Aided Design.
This software is used extensively by Civil, Mechanical and Architectural professionals, all across the globe.
Its main function is to draft any 3D structure in 2D or 3D schematic representation, with the exact dimensions, so as to show detailed information of every teeny-tiny curve of the structure.


General Rules:
Anyone wishing to participate must bring his/her own laptop. Software used will be AutoCAD 2014 and the setup will be provided by the organizers, 1 hour prior to the competition. Please bring your own Pen drives if you are going to need the setup.
The participants should check their system compatibility with the software. The organizers will not be responsible for any improper functioning of the software.
The event will consist of 2 rounds.
ROUND 1:
A prelim round, which would only be conducted if the no. of participants exceed our estimated batch.
For this, a simple 2D drawing would be given and some time would be allotted. Participants who finish the drawing within the allotted time period (i.e. 15 mins.) will proceed to the next round.
ROUND 2 :
The Final Round, for which, participants would be given a 3D plan. Time period for this round would be 45 minutes to 1 hour.
Since we have limited number of charging points, so first-come-first-serve basis would come into play!

PLOT IT
This event is based on the civil engineering and it is a team event. In this event you are provided with some questions to get the direction and with the help of the given directions you have to plot the traverse.

PRINCIPLE GUIDELINES:
This event will be conducted in two rounds.
ROUND 1:
It is a prelim round, in which the teams are asked to pass the Quiz which is related to the topic of Survey OR Civil Engineering.
And the 4 teams are qualified to the next round and they have to plot the traverse.
ROUND 2:
In this round the team are provided with the hint question and after that they are provided with the directions and they have to plot the traverse.
The team which complete the traverse first is awarded with the cash prize and the certificates.
JUDGING CRITERIA:
The team who complete the traverse first and made accurate angle with first reference point from the last point to complete the traverse.

QUIZ IT
It takes a great deal to make up a group to follow up your mark.
Here comes the war for you…the war of knowledge…the war of QUIZ
Do you have that panache in you!
Then Dare to take a QUIZ WAR challenge


Principle guidelines:
i. The quiz will be of two rounds.
ii. First round will be the qualification round where written test will be conducted.
iii. Top 12 applicants will be selected as per their score.
iv. The applicants will be paired as per the lottery system /mutual understanding for the final round.
v. In the final round there will be 5 teams. Each team consisting of three members.
vi. The questions will be regarding general affairs, current news, technical aspects.
vii. The final round will be conducted by quiz master. He/She will be asking the questions simultaneously.
viii. In the last round final decision will be of the person organizing the quiz.
ix. If the scores tally in the there will be tie breaker round between the teams having same score.
Judging Criteria:
i. In the first round the questions will be of mcq pattern.
ii. There will be 30 questions in first round. Each carrying 1 marks.
iii. 0.5 marks will be deducted for wrong answer in first round.
iv. In the buzzer round 25 questions will be there. Each carrying 10 marks for right answer. There will be negative of two marks for wrong answer.
v. In tie breaker round questions will be asked subsequently from the teams having same scores. The team which fails to give right answer will loose the match for the same rank.
vi. 10 marks for right answer and 3 marks will be deducted for wrong answer. However no deduction will be there for the pass question.
Topics of QUIZ WAR
i. Current affairs
ii. General Knowledge
iii. Technical Aspects
